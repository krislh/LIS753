External References

Cookbooks are wonderful resources for finding new recipes or even ideas for recipes. I have a list of cookbooks that I enjoy referring to when planning my meals.

Obviously this list is not exhaustive of all the cooking resources that I use, and I also have numerous family recipes and inherited cookbooks that are not availble for purchase anywhere. Like many people I'll also just search for specific recipes online. Pinterest is always fun for finding interesting ideas for recipes and meals.

  • Baking Yesteryear: The Best Recipes from the 1900s to the 1980s by B. Dylan Hollis
  • First We Eat: Good Food for Simple Gatherings from My Pacific Nortwest Kitchen
  • Budget Bytes: Over 100 Easy, Delicious Recipes to Slash Your Grocery Bill in Half by Beth Moncel

I also really like content developed by the America's Test Kitchen. Here is a list of some of my favorites

  • Cook It in Your Dutch Oven: 150 Foolproof Recipes Tailor-Made for Your Kitchen's Most Versatile Pot
  • The Complete Cooking for Two Cookbook: 650 Recipes for Everything You'll Ever Want to Make
  • Everyday Bread: 100 Recipes for Baking Bread on Your Schedule
  • The Perfect Cookie: Your Ultimate Guide to Foolproof Cookies, Brownies & Bars
  • Vegetables Illustrated: An Inspiring Guide with 700+ Kitchen-Tested Recipes
